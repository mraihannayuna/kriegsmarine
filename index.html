Kriegsmarine History

The Kriegsmarine (German pronunciation: [ˈkʁiːksmaˌʁiːnə], trans. the inter-war Reichsmarine period (1919–1935) of the Weimar Republic. Kriegsmarine was one of the three official branches, along with the Heer (Army) and Luftwaffe (Air Force) of the Wehrmacht, Germany's armed forces from 1933 to 1945. In violation of the Treaty of Versailles, Kriegsmarine grew rapidly during the war return to Germany in the 1930s. The 1919 treaty had previously limited the size of the German navy, and prohibited the building of submarines. [2] The Kriegsmarine was deployed to the waters around Spain during the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939) under the guise of enforcing non-intervention, but in reality supporting the Nationalist side against Spanish Republic. In January 1939, Plan Z signaled, calling for a naval balance with the British Royal Navy in 1944. When World War II broke out in September 1939, Plan Z was canceled in favor of a submarine (U-boat) building program instead of boats. major surface wars as well as giving strategic resource priority to ground and air forces. The commander-in-chief of the Kriegsmarine (as for all branches of the armed forces during the period of Nazi power) was "Führer" Adolf Hitler, who exercised his authority through the Oberkommando der Marine. The most significant of the Kriegsmarine ships were the U-boats, most of those built after Plan Z was aborted at the start of World War II. Wolfpack quickly put together the submarine groups that attacked British convoys during the first battle of the Atlantic but this tactic was largely abandoned in May 1943 as U-boat losses increased. Along with U-boats, surface merchant raiders (including auxiliary cruisers) were used to harass Allied shipping in the early years of the war, the most notable being the heavy cruisers Admiral Graf Spee and Admiral Scheer and the battleship Bismarck. However, the adoption of convoy guarding, especially in the Atlantic, greatly reduced the effectiveness of surface merchant ship raiders against convoys. After the Second World War in 1945, the remaining ships of the Kriegsmarine were divided among the Allied powers and used for various purposes including mine sweeping.
